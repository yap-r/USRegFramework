Methodology, assumptions and limitations

The tool converts five policy levers into six impact dimensions across four high-level stakeholder categories. Expandable rows show four model labs and three parts of the data center value chain. Each lever has a mechanism-specific sensitivity matrix; federal-state fragmentation adds a compliance penalty, and the decision horizon scales the degree of impact realization.

  • Impacts are relative, ordinal judgments across five directional bands—not expected returns or probabilities.
  • Competitive-position scores can be positive even when absolute industry economics are negative—for example, an incumbent may gain share while its total addressable market grows more slowly.
  • The “policy distance” label compares the selected mix with the July 2026 baseline; it is not a likelihood forecast.
  • High-intervention outcomes are deliberately severe and may require legislation, litigation outcomes, emergency authorities or a change in administration.
  • The tool is strategic decision support, not legal advice. Transaction-level analysis should separately assess CFIUS, export controls, antitrust and sector-specific rules.
